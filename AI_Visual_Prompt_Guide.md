# SMI Pilot Production: Episode 1 Visual Master Guide (2D Realistic Cartoon)

This guide is exclusively for **Episode 1: The Spark That Changed the World**. These prompts are engineered for **Luma AI (Dream Machine)** to create a clean, 2D realistic cartoon aesthetic with a heavy focus on objects and minimal backgrounds.

## Aesthetic DNA: "Realistic 2D Cartoon"
- **Style**: Clean line art, flat vector illustration, subtle cell-shading, high detail on technical components.
- **Backgrounds**: Minimalist, flat colors, or simple gradients to keep focus on the object.
- **Motion**: Smooth, purposeful movement of components and energy.

## PRO-LEVEL SPECIFICITY: "THE CINEMATIC ENGINE"
*Copy-paste these tokens to bridge the gap between 'good' and 'production-grade' visuals.*

### 1. The Lighting (The Mood)
*   **"Cinestill 800T lighting"**: Gives a moody, cinematic blue/green halation to highlights.
*   **"Volumetric God-rays"**: Visible light beams cutting through dust/smoke.
*   **"Soft-box studio lighting"**: Clean, professional technical product photography look.
*   **"High-contrast Chiaroscuro"**: Deep shadows and dramatic highlights for "The Ghost" Maxwell.

### 2. The Lens (The Focus)
*   **"35mm Prime Lens, f/1.8"**: Shallow depth of field; makes the main object pop while the background blurs.
*   **"Anamorphic lens flares"**: Cinematic horizontal blue flares for high-energy sparks.
*   **"Macro Photography Focus"**: Extreme detail on textures (metal scratches, dust on glass).

### 3. The Materiality (The Physics)
*   **"Micro-scratched brushed brass"**: Shows history and age on the equipment.
*   **"High-refractive glass"**: Realistic light bending through the spark gap spheres.
*   **"Subsurface Scattering"**: Makes the glowing blue spark look like physical plasma.

## GLOBAL STYLE SHEET (The SMI Aesthetic)
*Apply these parameters to EVERY Gemini/Luma prompt to ensure continuity.*

*   **Core Aesthetic**: "Realistic 2D Cartoon / Mid-Century Technical Illustration."
*   **Color Palette**: Aged Cream Background, Polished Brass Equipment, Electric Cobalt Blue Energy, Emerald Green UI.
*   **Camera Direction**: 
    *   **Diagrams**: Fixed Orthographic Side-View (Left-to-Right flow).
    *   **Action/People**: 3/4 Isometric View, Eye-level.
*   **Scale**: 
    *   **Technical**: Macro (Focus on specific parts, like the Spark Gap).
    *   **Establishing**: Wide (Focus on the environment, like the coastal towers).
*   **Positioning**: Rule of Thirds. The primary technical "action" (the spark/echo) must always occur in the Right-Third of the frame.

---

## KEYFRAME PAIRING STRATEGY
*To solve "Missing Animation" kinks (like a spark that never fires).*

**Instead of one prompt, we generate two:**
1.  **START FRAME**: The device at rest (The potential).
2.  **END FRAME**: The device at the peak of action (The spark/echo).
*Luma AI is then instructed to "Animate the transition from Frame A to Frame B."*
