### Science of Military Intelligence Key Concepts:
1.  **SOLAS (Safety of Life at Sea)**: The international treaty that mandates maritime safety equipment and procedures.
2.  **X-band (9 GHz)**: High-resolution radar used for coastal navigation and detecting small objects.
3.  **S-band (3 GHz)**: Long-range radar that can penetrate heavy rain and sea clutter better than X-band.
4.  **AIS (Automatic Identification System)**: A broadcast transponder system that shares vessel identity and position data.
5.  **Maritime Domain Awareness (MDA)**: The effective understanding of anything associated with the global maritime domain that could impact security, safety, economy, or environment.
6.  **VHF Data Link**: The radio frequency band used by AIS for short-range communication.
7.  **MarineTraffic/VesselFinder**: Public-facing websites that aggregate AIS data for global ship tracking.
8.  **The Radar Mile (12.36µs)**: The time it takes for a signal to travel one nautical mile and return.
9.  **Collision Avoidance (COLREGs)**: The 'Rules of the Road' for ships, which rely heavily on radar and AIS data.
10. **The Maxwell-Hertz Foundation**: The theoretical and experimental proof of electromagnetic waves that makes all modern radar and AIS possible.
