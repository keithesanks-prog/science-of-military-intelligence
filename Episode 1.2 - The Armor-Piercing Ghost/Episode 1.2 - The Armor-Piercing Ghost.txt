### Science of Military Intelligence Key Concepts:
1.  **X-Ray**: High-energy electromagnetic radiation with a wavelength shorter than UV light and longer than Gamma rays.
2.  **Vacuum Tube (Crookes Tube)**: A glass tube where a vacuum is maintained, allowing for the study of cathode rays.
3.  **Diffraction Foil**: The original tool Röntgen was using to study how electrons pass through materials.
4.  **Armor-Piercing (Hard) X-rays**: High-frequency X-rays used for industrial and military inspections of thick metals.
5.  **Gamma Rays**: The most energetic form of the EM spectrum, produced by nuclear decay and high-energy reactions.
6.  **NDT (Non-Destructive Testing)**: Techniques used to inspect materials without causing damage, essential for aerospace and submarine safety.
7.  **Nuclear Signature**: The specific emission of Gamma radiation used to detect and identify nuclear materials.
8.  **Penetration Depth**: The distance an EM wave can travel into a material before its intensity is significantly reduced.
9.  **Fluorescent Screen**: The material (often Barium Platinocyanide) that allowed Röntgen to 'see' the invisible X-ray leaks.
10. **The EM Spectrum**: The full range of electromagnetic radiation, from the 'Shout' of radio to the 'Punch' of Gamma rays.
