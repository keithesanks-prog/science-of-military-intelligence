### Science of Military Intelligence Key Concepts:
1.  **RCS (Radar Cross Section)**: The measure of how 'detectable' an object is by radar, measured in square meters.
2.  **Specular Reflection**: The 'mirror-like' reflection of energy off a flat surface.
3.  **ISAR (Inverse Synthetic Aperture Radar)**: A technique that uses the target's motion to create a high-resolution 2D image of its reflections.
4.  **Clean Deck Policy**: The naval design principle of moving all equipment inside the hull to minimize RCS.
5.  **The 90-Degree Corner (Dihedral)**: A shape that reflects energy directly back to its source, acting as a massive radar 'leak.'
6.  **Composite Materials**: Materials that absorb or transmit radar waves instead of reflecting them.
7.  **Energy Displacement**: The core principle of stealth—sending reflected energy away from the enemy's receiver.
8.  **Resonance**: When an object's size matches the radar's wavelength, causing it to glow brightly on screen.
9.  **Geometric Stealth**: Using flat, angled surfaces to control the direction of reflections (e.g., F-117).
10. **The Ghost vs. The Target**: The goal of stealth—to reduce the ghost until it is indistinguishable from the background noise.
