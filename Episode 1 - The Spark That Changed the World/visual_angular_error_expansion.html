The 360-Degree Problem

A ship at sea is the center of a 360-degree world. To survive, it needs to see every degree of that circle, every second. This is where the physics of the wave meets the reality of the clock.

The Beach Ball: The Wide Net (45°)

Sending out a beach-ball-sized wave is fast. It covers a huge area—about 45 degrees of your horizon in a single shout. But at one mile, that wedge is nearly a mile wide. You know a ship is there, but you don't know WHERE.

The Tennis Ball: The Scalpel (2.5°)

Our tennis balls—the decimeter waves—take more effort to coordinate, but the intelligence they return is surgical. This tight 2.5° beam allows you to pinpoint a single hull even ten miles out. This is Target for Action.
