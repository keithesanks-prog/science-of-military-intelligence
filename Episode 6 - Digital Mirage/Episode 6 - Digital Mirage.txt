### Mathematical Ghost Deep Dive: The Pulse-Echo Blueprint
(Visual: An animation of a clock hand spinning incredibly fast. A radio wave (The Shout) leaves a transmitter. It hits a plane and bounces back (The Echo).)

Narrator: "To find the distance, you have to stop shouting and start clicking. This is the math of Ranging. Radio waves travel at the speed of light—roughly 300 million meters every second. If you send a short 'pulse' and wait for the echo, you can calculate the range using a simple equation: **Range = (Speed of Light × Time) ÷ 2**."

(Visual: A graphic appears: **R = (c × t) / 2**. A scale shows 1 microsecond (1 millionth of a second).)

Narrator: "We divide by two because the wave has to travel there and back. In the world of the SMI, time is distance. We call this the **'Radar Mile.'** It takes roughly **12.36 microseconds** for a pulse to travel one nautical mile and return. If his 'ear' hears the echo at 12 microseconds, the ghost is one mile away. This is the blueprint for the precision of the modern world."

When you correlate the 'Cough' of the transmitter with the 'Shape' of the reflection, the Digital Mirage vanishes. You aren't just seeing a ghost; you're seeing the soul of the machine."

### Science of Military Intelligence Key Concepts:
1.  **Chaff**: Conductive strips (aluminum/metallized glass) cut to resonate with specific radar wavelengths.
2.  **Resonance**: The physical property where an object reflects maximum energy because its size matches the wave's tempo.
3.  **Active Jamming**: Using a high-power transmitter to overwhelm an enemy radar's receiver with noise.
4.  **Electronic Seduction**: The process of using a decoy to lure a missile's seeker away from its intended target.
5.  **MTV (Magnesium/Teflon/Viton)**: The standard pyrotechnic composition for high-intensity infrared flares.
6.  **Nulka**: An active, hovering rocket decoy that emulates a ship's radar cross-section to divert anti-ship missiles.
7.  **DRFM (Digital Radio Frequency Memory)**: Technology that records an incoming radar signal and re-transmits it to create a coherent false target.
8.  **HULTEC (Hull-to-Emitter Correlation)**: The intelligence process of associating a specific electronic emission with a specific platform or vessel.
9.  **SEI (Specific Emitter Identification)**: The technical method of identifying a unique transmitter by its unintentional signal characteristics.
10. **UMOP (Unintentional Modulation on Pulse)**: The subtle, inherent "fingerprint" in a radar pulse caused by hardware imperfections.
11. **ISAR (Inverse Synthetic Aperture Radar)**: Imaging technology that uses the ship's own motion to create a high-resolution structural "signature."
12. **The Radar Mile (12.36µs)**: The timing constant used to translate echo delay into physical distance.
